Research

Research Summary

My research interests span various topics in Empirical Asset Pricing: model-free and model-based analysis of higher-order risk premia in option markets, the study of non-parametric bounds on the stochastic discount factor, and topics in the study of high-frequency asset price variation. I favour an approach to research based on rigourously developed theoretical foundations.

Work in modern Empirical Asset Pricing is highly dependent on computational resources and programming ability. I can handle very large and computationally complex problems. I also make parts of my code publicly available, in hope to forward the ability of the scientific society to tackle complex problems.
